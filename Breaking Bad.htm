The series introduces the viewer to the life of Walter White (Brian Cranston), a 50-year-old chemistry school teacher from Albuquerque. In addition to teaching, he is forced to work out at a car wash to support his pregnant wife (Anna Gunn) and disabled son (Ar Jay Mitt). However, Walter has to reconsider his usual lifestyle when he is diagnosed with inoperable [5] lung cancer.In order to pay for the treatment and secure his family 's financial future, Walter White decides to engage in the production of methamphetamine. His partner is a former student, Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul). Together, they find a house on wheels and head to the desert to cook the first batch of the drug. But cooking is only half a lot, you still have to sell the methamphetamine you get. And along the way, partners get into difficult situations in which they have to make difficult decisions. For starters, Walter and Jesse have to get rid of the petty drug dealer Creezy-Ate who was going to kill them. Then there are problems in extracting the necessary amount of pseudoephedrine, and Walter White decides to change the original process of methamphetamine production. For that, he and Jesse steal a barrel of methylamine from a chemical warehouse. To market new portions of the drug, the partners reach an agreement with the local "distributor" Tuko Salamanca (Raymond Cruz). By their actions, aspiring criminals attract the attention of the DEA department led by Hank Schrader (Dean Norris), who is Walter White 's brother-in-law.
